Sinigang na Baka Recipe

Back to Home

Description

Sinigang na Baka is one of those timeless dishes that brings back memories of cold days and cozy meals. This hearty beef soup features a rich, tangy broth that pairs perfectly with tender meat and vegetables. If you’re craving something warm, comforting, and deeply familiar, this is a recipe worth making.

Ingredients

  • 4 lbs beef neck bones
  • 50 grams tamarind soup mix (Maggi Magic Sinigang)
  • 15 green beans, cut into 2 inch pieces
  • 5 taro roots, peeled
  • 2 Chinese eggplants, sliced diagonally
  • 3 long green peppers
  • 10 pieces okra
  • 1 white radish (daikon)
  • 4 plum tomatoes, wedged
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 onions, wedged
  • 1 quart rice water
  • 1 quart water
  • 1 bunch water spinach
  • 1 tablespoon beef powder
  • Fish sauce

Steps

  1. Sauté the aromatics – In a large pot, heat some oil and sauté the garlic, onions, and half of the tomatoes. Cook until the onions soften. This is your flavor base.
  2. Brown the beef – Add the beef neck bones and cook until the outer part starts to brown. This step helps bring out the beef flavor early.
  3. Simmer with rice water – Pour in the rice water and bring it to a boil. Once boiling, lower the heat and let it simmer until the meat becomes very tender. Skim off any foam or impurities from the top.
  4. Add the souring mix and root vegetables – Stir in the tamarind soup mix and beef powder. Add the taro and white radish. Let everything cook until the taro is soft.
  5. Add more vegetables – Add the rest of the tomatoes, the eggplant, and the green peppers. Let this cook for about 5 minutes. Then add the okra and green beans and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  6. Season and finish with leafy greens – Season the broth with fish sauce. Add the water spinach leaves last, letting them wilt for about a minute. Serve hot with steamed white rice.
